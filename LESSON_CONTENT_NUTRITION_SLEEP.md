# Nutrition & Sleep Optimization Modules - Lesson Content

## NUTRITION MODULE

### Lesson 1: Nutrition Fundamentals (20 min - Video)

**Learning Objectives:**
- Understand the role of nutrition in health and performance
- Learn about macronutrients and micronutrients
- Understand energy balance and metabolism
- Identify nutrition myths vs. facts

**Key Content:**
- Nutrition is the foundation of health, affecting energy, mood, cognition, and longevity
- Macronutrients: Protein (building blocks), Carbohydrates (energy), Fats (hormones, cell structure)
- Micronutrients: Vitamins and minerals essential for bodily functions
- Energy balance: Calories in vs. calories out determines weight change
- Metabolism basics: BMR, TDEE, and factors affecting metabolic rate
- Common myths: Carbs are bad, fat makes you fat, detoxes work, etc.

**Practice:** Calculate your TDEE and track food intake for 3 days

---

### Lesson 2: Macronutrients Explained (25 min - Video)

**Learning Objectives:**
- Deep dive into protein, carbohydrates, and fats
- Learn optimal intake ranges for each macronutrient
- Understand food sources and quality
- Learn how to balance macros for your goals

**Key Content:**
- **Protein:** 0.7-1g per lb bodyweight, essential amino acids, muscle repair, satiety
  - Sources: Meat, fish, eggs, dairy, legumes, tofu
- **Carbohydrates:** 45-65% of calories, primary energy source, fiber importance
  - Sources: Whole grains, fruits, vegetables, legumes
  - Simple vs. complex carbs, glycemic index
- **Fats:** 20-35% of calories, hormone production, nutrient absorption
  - Sources: Nuts, seeds, avocados, olive oil, fatty fish
  - Saturated, monounsaturated, polyunsaturated, trans fats
- Balancing macros for goals: fat loss, muscle gain, maintenance

**Practice:** Create a sample day of meals hitting your macro targets

### Lesson 4: Mindful Eating (12 min - Practice)

**Learning Objectives:**
- Understand hunger vs. emotional eating
- Practice mindful eating techniques
- Recognize fullness cues
- Develop a healthy relationship with food

**Key Content:**
- Hunger scale: 1 (starving) to 10 (stuffed), aim to eat at 3-4, stop at 6-7
- Emotional eating triggers: stress, boredom, sadness, celebration
- Mindful eating practice: Slow down, chew thoroughly, notice flavors and textures
- Remove distractions: No phone, TV, or computer while eating
- Check-in questions: Am I physically hungry? What do I really need?

**Practice:** Eat one meal per day mindfully for a week

**Learning Objectives:**
- Understand sustainable eating patterns
- Learn about flexible dieting

## SLEEP OPTIMIZATION MODULE

### Lesson 1: Sleep Science Basics (20 min - Video)

**Learning Objectives:**
- Understand sleep stages and cycles
- Learn the functions of sleep
- Recognize consequences of sleep deprivation
- Understand circadian rhythm

**Key Content:**
- **Sleep stages:**
  - Stage 1-2: Light sleep, transition
  - Stage 3: Deep sleep (physical recovery, immune function)
  - REM: Rapid eye movement (memory consolidation, emotional processing)
- **Sleep cycles:** 90-minute cycles, 4-6 cycles per night optimal
- **Functions of sleep:** Memory consolidation, toxin clearance, hormone regulation, immune function
- **Sleep deprivation effects:** Cognitive decline, mood issues, weakened immunity, weight gain
- **Circadian rhythm:** 24-hour internal clock, light-driven, affects alertness and sleep drive

**Practice:** Track sleep for 7 days (duration, quality, wake times)

---

### Lesson 2: Creating Your Sleep Routine (22 min - Video)

**Learning Objectives:**
- Design an optimal sleep schedule
- Create a wind-down routine
- Understand sleep hygiene principles
- Implement consistent sleep-wake times

**Key Content:**
- **Optimal sleep duration:** 7-9 hours for adults, individual variation
- **Consistent schedule:** Same bedtime and wake time, even weekends
- **Wind-down routine (60-90 minutes before bed):**
  - Dim lights (signals melatonin production)
  - Avoid screens (blue light suppresses melatonin)
  - Relaxing activities: Reading, stretching, meditation, warm bath
  - Temperature drop: Cool room (65-68°F optimal)
- **Sleep hygiene:**
  - Dark room (blackout curtains or eye mask)
  - Quiet (white noise if needed)
  - Cool temperature
  - Comfortable mattress and pillows
  - Reserve bed for sleep and intimacy only

**Practice:** Implement wind-down routine for 14 days

---

### Lesson 3: Sleep Environment Setup (15 min - Exercise)

**Learning Objectives:**
- Optimize bedroom for sleep
- Address environmental sleep disruptors
- Implement sleep-enhancing changes
- Create a sleep sanctuary

**Key Content:**
- **Light optimization:**
  - Blackout curtains or eye mask
  - Remove LED lights and electronics
  - Use red/amber night lights if needed
- **Sound optimization:**
  - White noise machine or fan
  - Earplugs if needed
  - Address external noise sources
- **Temperature:**
  - 65-68°F optimal
  - Breathable bedding
  - Cooling mattress pad if needed
- **Air quality:**
  - Good ventilation
  - Air purifier if needed
  - Plants for oxygen
- **Mattress and pillows:**
  - Replace mattress every 7-10 years
  - Supportive pillows for sleep position
  - Quality bedding

**Practice:** Complete sleep environment audit and make 3 improvements

### Lesson 4: Relaxation Techniques (18 min - Audio)

**Learning Objectives:**
- Practice progressive muscle relaxation
- Learn 4-7-8 breathing technique
- Use body scan meditation for sleep
- Implement visualization for relaxation

**Key Content:**
- **Progressive Muscle Relaxation (PMR):**
  - Tense and release each muscle group
  - Starts with feet, moves up to head
  - Releases physical tension
- **4-7-8 Breathing:**
  - Inhale 4 counts
  - Hold 7 counts
  - Exhale 8 counts
  - Repeat 4 times
  - Activates parasympathetic nervous system
- **Body Scan:**
  - Bring awareness to each body part
  - Notice sensations without judgment
  - Releases mental tension
- **Visualization:**
  - Imagine peaceful scene
  - Engage all senses
  - Distracts from racing thoughts

**Practice:** Use one technique nightly for 2 weeks

---

### Lesson 5: Advanced Sleep Strategies (25 min - Video)

**Learning Objectives:**
- Understand sleep supplements and medications
- Learn about sleep disorders
- Implement advanced optimization techniques
- Troubleshoot common sleep issues

**Key Content:**
- **Natural sleep aids:**
  - Magnesium glycinate (300-400mg)
  - L-theanine (200-400mg)
  - Glycine (3g)
  - Melatonin (0.3-1mg, not long-term)
- **Lifestyle factors:**
  - Exercise timing (not within 3 hours of bed)
  - Caffeine cutoff (8-10 hours before bed)
  - Alcohol impact (disrupts deep sleep and REM)
  - Large meals (finish 3 hours before bed)
- **Common sleep disorders:**
  - Insomnia: Difficulty falling or staying asleep
  - Sleep apnea: Breathing interruptions (seek medical help)
  - Restless leg syndrome
  - Circadian rhythm disorders
- **Troubleshooting:**
  - Can't fall asleep: Get up after 20 minutes, do relaxing activity
  - Wake in night: Don't check time, practice relaxation
  - Early waking: Evaluate stress, anxiety, or sleep debt
- **When to seek help:** Persistent issues >3 months, daytime impairment

**Practice:** Implement 3 advanced strategies for 30 days
