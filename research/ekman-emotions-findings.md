## Core Concept: What Are Emotions?

> "Emotions are a process, a particular kind of automatic appraisal influenced by our evolutionary and personal past, in which we sense that something important to our welfare is occurring, and a set of psychological changes and emotional behaviors begins to deal with the situation." - Paul Ekman, PhD

**Key Characteristics:**
- Emotions are AUTOMATIC (unbidden occurrence)
- We don't choose to feel them
- They prepare us to deal with important events without thinking
- Each emotion has distinctive signals, physiologies, and timelines
- Emotions typically don't last longer than an hour
- If they persist longer, they become moods or disorders

## Seven Universal Emotions

Ekman's research shows strongest evidence to date of **seven universal facial expressions** that transcend language, regional, cultural, and ethnic differences:

### 1. ANGER
- Universal facial expression
- Distinctive physiological signals
- Recognizable across all cultures

### 2. CONTEMPT
- Seventh emotion (added after original six)
- Strongest evidence to date
- Unique facial expression

### 3. DISGUST
- Core universal emotion
- Distinct facial markers
- Cross-cultural recognition

### 4. ENJOYMENT (Happiness)
- Universal positive emotion
- Clear facial signals
- Easily recognized globally

### 5. FEAR
- Survival-based emotion
- Distinctive facial expression
- Universal recognition

### 6. SADNESS
- Core negative emotion
- Clear facial indicators
- Cross-cultural understanding

### 7. SURPRISE
- Brief duration emotion
- Distinctive facial expression
- Universal recognition

## Important Concept: Emotion Families

**Critical Insight:** No emotion exists as a single affective or psychological state. Instead, emotions are comprised of a **family of related emotional states** which are variations on a shared theme.

**Example:** Anger isn't just "anger" - it includes:
- Irritation
- Frustration
- Rage
- Annoyance
- Fury
- Resentment

## What Makes Us Emotional?

Emotions occur in response to stimulus (actual, imagined, or re-lived):
- Physical events
- Social interactions
- Remembering or imagining events
- Talking about, thinking about, or physically reenacting past emotional experiences

**Individual Variation:** What makes us emotional varies person to person based on:
- Shared evolution
- Cultural influences
- Unique personal experiences

## Emotional Awareness

**Key Principle:** We can't choose the emotions we feel, but we CAN choose how we respond through emotional awareness.

**Process:**
1. Build awareness of emotional behaviors
2. Recognize emotional experiences
3. Identify emotional triggers (in ourselves and others)
4. Find insight in awareness
5. Shape emotional reactions in thoughtful and constructive ways

## Facial Action Coding System (FACS)

**What is FACS:**
- Comprehensive, anatomically based system
- Describes all visually discernible facial movement
- Published in 1978
- Used by thousands of scientists and researchers
- Gold standard for facial expression analysis

**How FACS Works:**
- Maps facial muscle movements to emotions
- Identifies micro-expressions (brief, involuntary facial expressions)
- Provides objective measurement of emotional displays
- Can detect concealed emotions

## Limitations and Considerations

**Important Notes:**
1. ✅ Seven emotions are universal across cultures
2. ✅ FACS provides objective measurement
3. ⚠️ What triggers emotions varies by individual
4. ⚠️ Cultural display rules affect expression (not feeling)
5. ⚠️ Must distinguish emotions (< 1 hour) from moods (longer)
6. ⚠️ Emotional awareness varies widely between individuals

**Ethical Considerations:**
- Clients must consent to facial analysis
- Privacy of emotional data
- Transparent about AI observation
- Coach maintains final interpretation authority
- AI provides prompts, not diagnoses
