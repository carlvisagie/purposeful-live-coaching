## Part 2: Leadership Styles Explained

### Autocratic Leadership
**Characteristics:** Makes decisions independently, provides clear direction, maintains strong control

**Strengths:** Quick decision-making, clear expectations, effective in crisis situations

**Challenges:** May limit creativity, can reduce team engagement, may create dependency

### Democratic Leadership
**Characteristics:** Involves team in decision-making, values input, builds consensus

**Strengths:** High team engagement, diverse perspectives, shared ownership

**Challenges:** Slower decision-making, may be challenging in urgent situations

### Transformational Leadership
**Characteristics:** Inspires and motivates, focuses on vision, encourages innovation

**Strengths:** High motivation, promotes growth, drives change

**Challenges:** May overlook details, requires high energy, can be overwhelming

### Servant Leadership
**Characteristics:** Focuses on team needs, empowers others, leads through service

**Strengths:** High trust, strong relationships, develops future leaders

**Challenges:** May be seen as weak, can be taken advantage of, may neglect own needs

### Laissez-Faire Leadership
**Characteristics:** Minimal direct supervision, high autonomy, trust-based

**Strengths:** Promotes independence, encourages creativity, empowers experts

**Challenges:** May lack direction, can lead to confusion, requires highly skilled team

### Situational Leadership
**Characteristics:** Adapts style to situation, flexible approach, context-aware

**Strengths:** Highly effective, responsive to needs, develops versatility

**Challenges:** Requires high awareness, can be inconsistent, demands skill
