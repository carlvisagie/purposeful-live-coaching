# Circadian Assessment Quiz: Discover Your Chronotype

## Purpose and Instructions

This **Circadian Assessment Quiz** is based on the scientifically validated **Morningness-Eveningness Questionnaire (MEQ)**, developed by Horne and Östberg [1]. Its purpose is to help you determine your natural **chronotype**—your innate preference for morning or evening activity. Understanding your chronotype is the first step toward aligning your daily schedule (sleep, work, exercise, meals) with your body's internal clock, a process known as **circadian alignment**. This alignment is crucial for optimizing energy, mood, cognitive performance, and long-term health.

### How to Complete This Assessment

1.  **Answer Honestly:** Respond to each of the 19 questions based on your natural preferences, not your current schedule (which may be forced by external commitments).
2.  **Mark Your Choice:** For each question, place an `[x]` in the box next to the answer that best describes you.
3.  **Record Your Score:** After selecting an answer, note the corresponding point value in the `[Points]` column.
4.  **Calculate Total:** Sum all 19 scores to find your final **MEQ Score**.

## Scoring and Interpretation

**Total MEQ Score:** ____________________ (Sum of all 19 points)

Your total score will fall between a minimum of 16 and a maximum of 86. Use the table below to determine your chronotype.

| MEQ Score Range | Chronotype | Description |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **70 - 86** | **Definitely Morning Type** | You have a strong preference for waking and being active early in the day. Your peak performance occurs in the morning. |
| **59 - 69** | **Moderately Morning Type** | You lean towards morning activity and alertness, but with some flexibility. |
| **42 - 58** | **Neither Type (Intermediate)** | You are flexible and can adapt to both morning and evening schedules without a strong preference for either. |
| **31 - 41** | **Moderately Evening Type** | You lean towards evening activity and alertness, with peak performance occurring later in the day. |
| **16 - 30** | **Definitely Evening Type** | You have a strong preference for waking and being active late in the day. Your peak performance occurs in the evening. |

## Action Steps for Circadian Alignment

Based on your results, here are some evidence-based steps you can take to improve your circadian alignment and overall well-being.

### 1. Light Exposure Management

*   **Morning Light:** Get at least **10-30 minutes of bright, natural light** as soon as possible after waking up (ideally within the first hour). This is the single most powerful signal to reset your master clock.
*   **Daytime Light:** Maximize natural light exposure during the day. Work near a window or take short walks outside.
*   **Evening Light:** Minimize blue light exposure (from screens, phones, tablets) for **2-3 hours before bed**. Blue light suppresses melatonin, delaying sleep. Use blue-light filtering glasses or apps.

### 2. Consistent Sleep Schedule

*   **Fixed Wake Time:** The most critical step is to maintain a **consistent wake-up time**, even on weekends. This anchors your circadian rhythm.
*   **Listen to Your Body:** Use your MEQ result to identify your natural sleep window. If you are an **Evening Type**, try to shift your schedule earlier gradually (e.g., 15 minutes per week). If you are a **Morning Type**, ensure you are not staying up too late.

### 3. Meal Timing

*   **Time-Restricted Eating:** Try to eat your meals within a consistent 8-12 hour window. **Avoid eating 2-3 hours before bed**, as digestion can interfere with sleep and circadian signaling.
*   **Breakfast is Key:** Eat a protein-rich breakfast soon after waking to further signal the start of your biological day.

### 4. Exercise Timing

*   **Morning/Afternoon Exercise:** For most people, exercise in the late morning or early afternoon is optimal for performance and body temperature regulation.
*   **Avoid Late-Night Exercise:** Intense exercise too close to bedtime can raise your core body temperature and make it harder to fall asleep.
