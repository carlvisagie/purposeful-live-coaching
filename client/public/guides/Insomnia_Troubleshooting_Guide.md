# Insomnia Troubleshooting Guide

**Module:** Health & Wellness

**Purpose:** To provide a guide to troubleshooting common sleep problems and to help you to get a better night's sleep.

**Instructions:** This guide provides a list of common sleep problems and their possible solutions. If you are still having trouble sleeping after trying these tips, it is recommended that you seek support from a healthcare professional.

---

### Common Sleep Problems and Solutions

| Problem | Possible Causes | Possible Solutions |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Trouble Falling Asleep** | Stress, anxiety, caffeine, screen time before bed | Create a relaxing bedtime routine, avoid caffeine and alcohol before bed, avoid screens before bed, practice relaxation techniques |
| **Waking Up in the Middle of the Night** | Noise, light, temperature, alcohol, sleep apnea | Create a comfortable sleep environment, avoid alcohol before bed, talk to your doctor about sleep apnea |
| **Waking Up Too Early** | Depression, anxiety, sleep apnea | Talk to your doctor about depression and anxiety, talk to your doctor about sleep apnea |
| **Feeling Tired After a Full Night's Sleep** | Poor sleep quality, sleep apnea, other medical conditions | Improve your sleep quality, talk to your doctor about sleep apnea, talk to your doctor about other medical conditions |

### Tips for Better Sleep

*   **Create a relaxing bedtime routine:** Take a warm bath, read a book, or listen to calming music before bed.
*   **Stick to a regular sleep schedule:** Go to bed and wake up at the same time each day, even on weekends.
*   **Create a comfortable sleep environment:** Make sure your bedroom is dark, quiet, and cool.
*   **Avoid caffeine and alcohol before bed:** These substances can interfere with your sleep.
*   **Get regular exercise:** Exercise can help you to fall asleep more easily and to sleep more soundly.
*   **Avoid screens before bed:** The blue light from screens can interfere with your body's production of melatonin, a hormone that helps you to sleep.

---

### When to See a Doctor

If you are still having trouble sleeping after trying these tips, it is recommended that you seek support from a healthcare professional. You may have a sleep disorder, such as sleep apnea or insomnia, that requires medical treatment.
