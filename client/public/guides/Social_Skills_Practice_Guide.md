# Social Skills Practice Guide

**Module:** Relationships

**Purpose:** To provide a guide to practicing and improving your social skills.

**Instructions:** This guide provides tips and exercises for practicing your social skills. The more you practice, the more confident you will become.

---

### Tips for Effective Social Skills

*   **Be a good listener:** Pay attention to what the other person is saying, and try to understand their perspective. Ask open-ended questions to keep the conversation going.
*   **Be interested in others:** People are more likely to be interested in you if you are interested in them. Ask people about their hobbies, their work, and their families.
*   **Be positive and enthusiastic:** A positive attitude is contagious. People are more likely to be drawn to you if you are positive and enthusiastic.
*   **Be yourself:** Don't try to be someone you're not. The more authentic you are, the more people will connect with you.
*   **Practice, practice, practice:** The more you practice your social skills, the more confident you will become.

### Social Skills Practice Exercises

*   **Start a conversation with a stranger:** This can be as simple as saying hello to someone in the grocery store or asking someone for directions.
*   **Join a club or group:** This is a great way to meet new people who share your interests.
*   **Volunteer:** Volunteering is a great way to meet new people and to make a difference in your community.
*   **Take a class:** Take a class on a topic that interests you. This is a great way to meet new people and to learn something new.
*   **Practice with a friend:** Ask a friend to practice your social skills with you. You can role-play different social situations, such as starting a conversation or asking someone out on a date.
