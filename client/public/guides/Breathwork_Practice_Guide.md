# Breathwork Practice Guide

**Module:** Health & Wellness

**Purpose:** To provide a guide to various breathwork practices for stress reduction, increased energy, and improved focus.

**Instructions:** This guide provides instructions for several different breathwork practices. Experiment with each of them to see which ones work best for you. It is best to practice these techniques in a quiet, comfortable space.

---

### 1. Box Breathing

*A simple and powerful technique for calming the nervous system.*

1.  Inhale slowly for a count of four.
2.  Hold your breath for a count of four.
3.  Exhale slowly for a count of four.
4.  Hold your breath for a count of four.
5.  Repeat for 5-10 cycles.

### 2. 4-7-8 Breathing

*A relaxing breath technique that can help you to fall asleep more easily.*

1.  Exhale completely through your mouth, making a whoosh sound.
2.  Close your mouth and inhale quietly through your nose to a mental count of four.
3.  Hold your breath for a count of seven.
4.  Exhale completely through your mouth, making a whoosh sound to a count of eight.
5.  This is one breath. Now inhale again and repeat the cycle three more times for a total of four breaths.

### 3. Diaphragmatic Breathing (Belly Breathing)

*A foundational breathwork practice that encourages deep, relaxing breaths.*

1.  Lie on your back with your knees bent and your feet flat on the floor. You can also practice this sitting in a chair.
2.  Place one hand on your upper chest and the other on your belly, just below your rib cage.
3.  Breathe in slowly through your nose, allowing your belly to rise as you inhale. The hand on your chest should remain as still as possible.
4.  Exhale slowly through your mouth, allowing your belly to fall. The hand on your chest should remain as still as possible.
5.  Continue for 5-10 minutes.

### 4. Alternate Nostril Breathing (Nadi Shodhana)

*A traditional yogic breathing technique that is said to balance the two hemispheres of the brain.*

1.  Sit in a comfortable position with your spine straight.
2.  Close your right nostril with your right thumb and inhale slowly through your left nostril.
3.  Close your left nostril with your right ring finger, so both nostrils are held closed. Hold your breath for a moment.
4.  Open your right nostril and exhale slowly.
5.  Inhale slowly through your right nostril.
6.  Close your right nostril with your right thumb. Hold your breath for a moment.
7.  Open your left nostril and exhale slowly.
8.  This is one round. Continue for 5-10 rounds.

### 5. Wim Hof Method Breathing

*A powerful breathing technique that can increase energy, reduce stress, and improve focus.*

1.  Sit or lie down in a comfortable position.
2.  Take 30-40 deep, powerful breaths. Inhale through your nose or mouth and exhale through your mouth in short, powerful bursts.
3.  After the last exhalation, inhale one final time, as deeply as you can. Then, let the air out and stop breathing. Hold your breath for as long as you can.
4.  When you feel the urge to breathe, take one deep breath in and hold it for 15-20 seconds. Then, exhale.
5.  This is one round. You can repeat the cycle 3-4 times.

---

### Cautions

*   If you are pregnant, have a history of cardiovascular problems, or have any other health concerns, please consult with your doctor before practicing these breathwork techniques.
*   If you feel dizzy or lightheaded at any point, stop the practice and breathe normally.
