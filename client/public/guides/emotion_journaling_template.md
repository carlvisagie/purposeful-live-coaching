## The Emotion Wheel

Use this to identify nuanced emotions:

**Happy Family:**
- Joyful, Grateful, Peaceful, Powerful, Accepted, Proud, Interested, Content, Playful

**Sad Family:**
- Lonely, Vulnerable, Despair, Guilty, Depressed, Hurt

**Angry Family:**
- Let down, Humiliated, Bitter, Mad, Aggressive, Frustrated, Distant, Critical

**Fearful Family:**
- Scared, Anxious, Insecure, Weak, Rejected, Threatened

**Surprised Family:**
- Startled, Confused, Amazed, Excited

**Disgusted Family:**
- Disapproving, Disappointed, Awful, Repelled

---

## Tips for Effective Emotion Journaling

1. **Be specific:** Instead of "bad," identify the exact emotion (frustrated, disappointed, overwhelmed)
2. **Notice patterns:** Track recurring emotional triggers
3. **No judgment:** All emotions are valid information
4. **Include body sensations:** Emotions have physical components
5. **Track intensity:** Rate emotions 1-10 to notice changes
6. **Review weekly:** Look for patterns and progress
