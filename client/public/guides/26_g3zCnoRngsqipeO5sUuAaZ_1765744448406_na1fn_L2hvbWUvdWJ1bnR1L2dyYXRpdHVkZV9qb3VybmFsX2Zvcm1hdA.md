# Professional Gratitude Journal Format: A Tool for Enhanced Well-being and Performance

**Category: Template**

## 1. Purpose and Evidence-Based Rationale

The practice of gratitude journaling is a powerful, evidence-based intervention designed to shift cognitive focus from scarcity and stress to abundance and appreciation [1]. Regular engagement with this practice has been scientifically linked to a host of positive outcomes, including:

*   **Increased Subjective Well-being:** Higher levels of happiness and life satisfaction [2].
*   **Improved Physical Health:** Stronger immune systems and better sleep quality [3].
*   **Enhanced Resilience:** Greater ability to cope with stress and adversity [4].
*   **Better Decision-Making:** A more positive outlook that supports rational and optimistic choices.

This template is designed for coaching clients to integrate this practice seamlessly into their daily routine, transforming a simple exercise into a structured tool for personal and professional growth.

## 2. Instructions for Optimal Use

To maximize the benefits of this journal, commit to a consistent practice, ideally at the same time each day (e.g., before bed or first thing in the morning).

| Step | Instruction | Check |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1. Set the Scene** | Find a quiet space and dedicate 5-10 minutes without distraction. | [ ] |
| **2. Be Specific** | Move beyond general statements. Instead of "I'm grateful for my family," write "I'm grateful for the specific conversation I had with my daughter about her new project." | [ ] |
| **3. Focus on People** | Include at least one person and describe the positive impact they had on your day. | [ ] |
| **4. Record the Feeling** | Note how the gratitude made you feel (e.g., calm, energized, connected). | [ ] |
| **5. Review Weekly** | Use the reflection questions to integrate the week's insights into future actions. | [ ] |

## 4. Weekly Reflection and Integration

Complete this section once per week to synthesize your entries and translate insights into actionable steps.

### Reflection Questions

1.  **Pattern Recognition:** Looking back at the last seven entries, what is the most common theme or source of gratitude?
    (Write your answer here)

2.  **Unexpected Gratitude:** What was one thing you were grateful for that surprised you?
    (Write your answer here)

3.  **The Missing Piece:** What area of your life (e.g., career, health) is currently underrepresented in your log, and why?
    (Write your answer here)

### Action Steps for the Coming Week

Use your reflections to set an intention for the next seven days.

| Action Step | Status |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Intention Setting:** I will intentionally seek out and notice moments of gratitude related to (Specific Area/Goal). | [ ] |
| **Behavioral Change:** I will express my gratitude directly to (Person/Group) by (Specific Action). | [ ] |
| **Mindfulness Practice:** I will pause for 30 seconds before every meal to acknowledge the effort and resources involved. | [ ] |
