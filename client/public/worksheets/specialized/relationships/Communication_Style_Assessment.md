# Communication Style Assessment

**Module:** Relationships

**Purpose:** To help you to understand your own communication style and to learn how to communicate more effectively with others.

**Instructions:** This assessment will help you to identify your dominant communication style. There are no right or wrong answers. Be honest with yourself as you answer the questions.

---

### Part 1: The Assessment

*For each of the following statements, choose the response that best describes you.*

**1. When I have a problem, I am most likely to:**

a) Keep it to myself.
b) Hint at it and hope someone notices.
c) Tell someone directly what is wrong.
d) Blame someone else for the problem.

**2. When I disagree with someone, I am most likely to:**

a) Go along with what they want.
b) Say nothing, but be resentful later.
c) Express my opinion clearly and respectfully.
d) Try to win the argument at all costs.

**3. When I need something from someone, I am most likely to:**

a) Hope they will offer it to me.
b) Complain about my situation.
c) Ask for what I need directly.
d) Demand that they give it to me.

**4. When someone criticizes me, I am most likely to:**

a) Feel hurt and withdraw.
b) Make excuses or become defensive.
c) Listen to their feedback and consider it.
d) Criticize them back.

**5. When I am angry, I am most likely to:**

a) Suppress my anger and pretend everything is fine.
b) Give someone the silent treatment.
c) Express my anger in a calm and controlled way.
d) Yell or slam doors.

### Part 2: Scoring

*Count the number of times you chose each letter.*

*   **Mostly A's:** You have a **passive** communication style.
*   **Mostly B's:** You have a **passive-aggressive** communication style.
*   **Mostly C's:** You have an **assertive** communication style.
*   **Mostly D's:** You have an **aggressive** communication style.

### Part 3: Understanding Your Style

*   **Passive:** You tend to avoid conflict and put the needs of others before your own. You may have difficulty saying no and expressing your own opinions and feelings.
*   **Passive-Aggressive:** You tend to express your anger and frustration indirectly, through sarcasm, procrastination, or the silent treatment. You may have difficulty being direct and honest about your feelings.
*   **Assertive:** You are able to express your own opinions and feelings clearly and respectfully, while also listening to and respecting the opinions and feelings of others. You are able to say no without feeling guilty and to ask for what you need directly.
*   **Aggressive:** You tend to express your opinions and feelings in a way that is disrespectful or intimidating to others. You may have difficulty listening to others and may try to control or dominate conversations.

### Part 4: Developing a More Assertive Communication Style

*Assertive communication is the healthiest and most effective communication style. Here are some tips for developing a more assertive communication style:*

*   **Use "I" statements:** Instead of saying "You always..." or "You never...", say "I feel..." or "I need..."
*   **Be clear and direct:** Don't hint at what you want or need. Ask for it directly.
*   **Be respectful:** Even when you disagree with someone, be respectful of their opinions and feelings.
*   **Listen actively:** Pay attention to what the other person is saying, and try to understand their perspective.
*   **Practice saying no:** It's okay to say no to things you don't want to do. You don't have to make excuses or feel guilty.
