# Sleep Quality Assessment

**Module:** Health & Wellness

**Purpose:** To help you to assess the quality of your sleep and to identify areas for improvement.

**Instructions:** This is not a diagnostic tool. It is a self-assessment to help you to become more aware of your sleep habits. Answer the following questions honestly and without judgment.

---

### Part 1: The Assessment

*For each of the following statements, choose the response that best describes you.*

**1. How many hours of sleep do you get on an average night?**

a) Less than 5 hours
b) 5-6 hours
c) 7-8 hours
d) More than 9 hours

**2. How long does it usually take you to fall asleep?**

a) Less than 15 minutes
b) 15-30 minutes
c) 30-60 minutes
d) More than 60 minutes

**3. How many times do you wake up during the night?**

a) 0-1 times
b) 2-3 times
c) 4-5 times
d) More than 5 times

**4. How do you feel when you wake up in the morning?**

a) Refreshed and energized.
b) Somewhat rested.
c) Tired and groggy.
d) Exhausted.

**5. How is your energy level during the day?**

a) Consistently high.
b) Generally good, with a few slumps.
c) Often low.
d) Consistently low.

### Part 2: Scoring

*Give yourself 1 point for each "a" answer, 2 points for each "b" answer, 3 points for each "c" answer, and 4 points for each "d" answer. Add up your total score.*

*   **5-8:** You are likely getting good quality sleep.
*   **9-12:** There is room for improvement in your sleep quality.
*   **13-16:** You may be experiencing significant challenges with your sleep.
*   **17-20:** It is highly recommended that you seek support from a healthcare professional to address your sleep concerns.

### Part 3: Improving Your Sleep Quality

*Here are some tips for improving your sleep quality:*

*   **Create a relaxing bedtime routine:** Take a warm bath, read a book, or listen to calming music before bed.
*   **Stick to a regular sleep schedule:** Go to bed and wake up at the same time each day, even on weekends.
*   **Create a comfortable sleep environment:** Make sure your bedroom is dark, quiet, and cool.
*   **Avoid caffeine and alcohol before bed:** These substances can interfere with your sleep.
*   **Get regular exercise:** Exercise can help you to fall asleep more easily and to sleep more soundly.
*   **Avoid screens before bed:** The blue light from screens can interfere with your body's production of melatonin, a hormone that helps you to sleep.
