# Money Mindset Assessment

**Module:** Financial & Career

**Purpose:** To help you to understand your beliefs and attitudes about money, and to identify any limiting beliefs that may be holding you back from achieving your financial goals.

**Instructions:** This assessment will help you to identify your dominant money mindset. There are no right or wrong answers. Be honest with yourself as you answer the questions.

---

### Part 1: The Assessment

*For each of the following statements, choose the response that best describes you.*

**1. When I think about money, I feel:**

a) Anxious and stressed.
b) Excited and optimistic.
c) Indifferent.
d) Confused and overwhelmed.

**2. I believe that money is:**

a) The root of all evil.
b) A tool that can be used for good.
c) Something that is not important to me.
d) Something that is scarce and hard to come by.

**3. When it comes to my finances, I am:**

a) Avoident and prefer not to think about them.
b) Proactive and engaged.
c) Passive and let things happen as they may.
d) Reactive and only deal with problems as they arise.

**4. I believe that my ability to earn money is:**

a) Limited and fixed.
b) Unlimited and can be expanded.
c) Dependent on luck and circumstances.
d) Something I have little control over.

**5. When I have extra money, I am most likely to:**

a) Spend it right away.
b) Save or invest it.
c) Give it away.
d) Worry about losing it.

### Part 2: Scoring

*Count the number of times you chose each letter.*

*   **Mostly A's:** You have a **scarcity** money mindset.
*   **Mostly B's:** You have an **abundance** money mindset.
*   **Mostly C's:** You have an **indifferent** money mindset.
*   **Mostly D's:** You have a **fear-based** money mindset.

### Part 3: Understanding Your Mindset

*   **Scarcity Mindset:** You believe that there is not enough money to go around, and that you will never have enough. This can lead to feelings of anxiety, fear, and envy.
*   **Abundance Mindset:** You believe that there is plenty of money to go around, and that you have the ability to create the wealth you desire. This can lead to feelings of optimism, gratitude, and generosity.
*   **Indifferent Mindset:** You believe that money is not important, and you may neglect your finances as a result. This can lead to financial problems down the road.
*   **Fear-Based Mindset:** You are afraid of losing money, and you may be overly cautious with your financial decisions. This can prevent you from taking calculated risks that could lead to financial growth.

### Part 4: Developing an Abundance Mindset

*An abundance mindset is the most empowering and effective money mindset. Here are some tips for developing an abundance mindset:*

*   **Practice gratitude:** Be grateful for the money you have, no matter how much or how little.
*   **Focus on opportunities:** Instead of focusing on what you lack, focus on the opportunities that are available to you.
*   **Surround yourself with abundance:** Spend time with people who have an abundance mindset, and read books and articles about wealth and abundance.
*   **Give generously:** When you give freely, you send a message to the universe that you believe there is more than enough to go around.
*   **Take calculated risks:** Don't be afraid to take calculated risks that could lead to financial growth.
