# Anxiety Reduction Toolkit

**Module:** Mental Health

**Purpose:** To provide a collection of evidence-based techniques for managing anxiety in the moment.

**Instructions:** This toolkit contains a variety of exercises that can help you to reduce anxiety and calm your nervous system. Experiment with different techniques to find what works best for you. Practice these techniques regularly, even when you are not feeling anxious, so that they will be more effective when you need them most.

---

### 1. Box Breathing

*A simple and powerful breathing technique to calm your nervous system.*

1.  Inhale slowly for a count of four.
2.  Hold your breath for a count of four.
3.  Exhale slowly for a count of four.
4.  Hold your breath for a count of four.
5.  Repeat for 5-10 cycles.

### 2. 5-4-3-2-1 Grounding Technique

*A mindfulness exercise to bring you back to the present moment.*

*   **5:** Name five things you can see.
*   **4:** Name four things you can feel.
*   **3:** Name three things you can hear.
*   **2:** Name two things you can smell.
*   **1:** Name one thing you can taste.

### 3. Progressive Muscle Relaxation

*A technique to release physical tension and promote relaxation.*

1.  Find a comfortable position and take a few deep breaths.
2.  Starting with your toes, tense the muscles for a count of five, then release.
3.  Work your way up your body, tensing and releasing each muscle group: calves, thighs, glutes, abdomen, chest, back, arms, hands, shoulders, neck, and face.
4.  Notice the difference between tension and relaxation in each muscle group.

### 4. Worry Time

*A cognitive technique to contain your worries and prevent them from taking over your life.*

1.  Schedule a specific time each day (e.g., 15 minutes at 5:00 PM) to worry.
2.  When you notice yourself worrying outside of your scheduled worry time, jot down the worry and postpone it until your worry time.
3.  During your worry time, allow yourself to worry freely about the items on your list.
4.  When your worry time is over, let go of the worries and move on with your day.

### 5. Self-Compassion Break

*A practice to offer yourself kindness and support when you are feeling anxious.*

1.  **Acknowledge the anxiety:** "This is a moment of anxiety."
2.  **Connect with common humanity:** "Anxiety is a part of life."
3.  **Offer yourself kindness:** "May I be kind to myself in this moment."
