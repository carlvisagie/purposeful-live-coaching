# Exercise Form Guide

**Module:** Physical Fitness

**Purpose:** To provide a visual guide to proper form for fundamental exercises.

**Instructions:** Use this guide to learn and practice proper form for the squat, deadlift, push-up, and bent-over row. Focus on mastering the form before adding weight. Consider recording yourself to check your form against these cues.

---

### The Squat

**Key Cues:**

*   **Stance:** Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart, or slightly wider, with your toes pointed slightly out.
*   **Core:** Keep your core engaged and your chest up throughout the movement.
*   **Hips:** Initiate the movement by pushing your hips back, as if you are sitting in a chair.
*   **Knees:** Keep your knees in line with your toes. Do not let them collapse inward.
*   **Depth:** Lower your hips until they are parallel to or below your knees.
*   **Drive:** Drive through your heels to return to the starting position.

### The Deadlift

**Key Cues:**

*   **Stance:** Stand with your feet hip-width apart, with the middle of your feet under the barbell.
*   **Grip:** Grip the bar just outside your shins. Use a double overhand or mixed grip.
*   **Back:** Keep your back straight and your chest up. Do not round your lower back.
*   **Hips:** Hinge at your hips, keeping your hips higher than your knees.
*   **Lift:** Drive through your heels and lift the bar by extending your hips and knees. Keep the bar close to your body.
*   **Lower:** Lower the bar with control by hinging at your hips.

### The Push-Up

**Key Cues:**

*   **Hand Position:** Place your hands slightly wider than shoulder-width apart.
*   **Body:** Keep your body in a straight line from your head to your heels. Do not let your hips sag or rise.
*   **Core:** Engage your core and glutes to maintain a stable spine.
*   **Elbows:** Lower your body until your chest is just above the floor, keeping your elbows at a 45-degree angle to your body.
*   **Push:** Push through your hands to return to the starting position.

### The Bent-Over Row

**Key Cues:**

*   **Stance:** Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart, with a slight bend in your knees.
*   **Hinge:** Hinge at your hips until your torso is nearly parallel to the floor. Keep your back straight.
*   **Grip:** Grip the barbell with a slightly wider than shoulder-width grip.
*   **Pull:** Pull the barbell up to your lower chest, squeezing your shoulder blades together.
*   **Elbows:** Keep your elbows tucked in at a 45-degree angle to your body.
*   **Lower:** Lower the barbell with control to the starting position.
