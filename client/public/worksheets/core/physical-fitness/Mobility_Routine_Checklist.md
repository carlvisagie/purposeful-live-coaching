# Mobility Routine Checklist

**Module:** Physical Fitness

**Purpose:** To improve flexibility, reduce the risk of injury, and enhance recovery.

**Instructions:** Perform this mobility routine 3-5 times per week, either as a warm-up before your workouts or as a standalone session. Hold each stretch for 30-60 seconds, and do not stretch to the point of pain.

### Exercise Descriptions

*   **Cat-Cow:** Start on your hands and knees. Inhale as you drop your belly and look up (Cow). Exhale as you round your spine and tuck your chin (Cat).
*   **Thoracic Spine Windmills:** Lie on your side with your knees bent and your arms extended in front of you. Keeping your hips stacked, rotate your top arm up and over to the other side, following your hand with your eyes.
*   **World's Greatest Stretch:** Step into a lunge, placing your hands on the floor inside your front foot. Rotate your torso and reach your top arm to the ceiling. Return to the starting position and repeat on the other side.
*   **Pigeon Pose:** From a push-up position, bring one knee forward and place it behind your wrist. Extend your other leg straight back. You should feel a stretch in the hip of your front leg.
*   **Couch Stretch:** Place one knee on the floor and the top of your foot against a wall or couch. Step your other foot forward into a lunge. Keep your torso upright and your core engaged.
*   **Doorway Chest Stretch:** Stand in a doorway and place your forearms on the frame, with your elbows slightly below your shoulders. Step forward with one foot until you feel a stretch in your chest.
*   **Child's Pose:** Kneel on the floor and sit back on your heels. Fold forward and rest your forehead on the floor, with your arms extended in front of you or resting alongside your body.
*   **Deep Squat Hold:** Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart and lower your hips as far as you can, keeping your chest up and your heels on the floor.
